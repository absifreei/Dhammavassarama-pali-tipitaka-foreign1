勝鬘經 [py] Sheng4man2 jing1 [wg] Sheng-man ching [ko] Sûngmangyông [ja] ショウマンギョウ Shoumangyou ||| Skt. `Sriimaalaa-suutra. The full title of this text is Shengman shizi hu yisheng da fangbian fangguang jing 勝鬘師子吼一乘大方便方廣經 (`Sriimaalaadevii-simhanaada-suutra); trans. to Chinese in 436 CE by Gunabhadra 求那跋陀羅 (394-468). T 353.12.217a-223b. This sutra is one of the most representative of the Mahaayaana texts which teach the theories of tathaagatagarbha 如來藏and the Single Vehicle 一乘 (in this case, through the words of Queen `Sriimaalaa). Because of the nature of its contents, it is often discussed in comparison with such texts as the Jewel Nature Treatise, the Lankaavataara-suutra 楞伽經, the Awakening of Mahaayaana Faith 大乘起信論, etc. The text has been translated into English by Alex and Hideko Wayman with the title The Lion's Roar of Queen Srimala. An older version of this text was known by the title of 夫人經. [Dictionary References] bkj92b [Credit] acm(entry) cw(py)
