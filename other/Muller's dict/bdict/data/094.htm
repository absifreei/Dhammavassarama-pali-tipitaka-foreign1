獨立論證派 [py] du2li4lun4zheng4pai4 [wg] tu-li-lun-cheng-p'ai [ko] tok'ipnonjûngp'a [ja] ドクリツロンショウハ dokuritsuronshouha ||| The Svaatrantika school, which was a branch of Indian Maadhyamika 中觀派 which developed out of the writings of the the Middle Way scholar Bhaavaviveka 清辯(ca. 490-570). Bhaavaviveka was known for his use of ositive dialectic to support the theory of emptiness. In this he was distinguished from philosophical opponents such as Buddhapaalita 佛護 and Candrakiirti 月稱, both of whom denied the validity of the use of logical propositions which ended up affirming any sort of positive position. Their theories would result in the development of an opposing school called Praasa.ngika (Guimiulunzhengpai 歸謬論證派)。 [Credit] acm(entry)
