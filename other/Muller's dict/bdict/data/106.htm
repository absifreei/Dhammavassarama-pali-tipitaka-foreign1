百法 [py] bai3fa3 [wg] paifa [ko] paekpôp [ja] ヒャッポウ hyappou ||| 'One Hundred Dharmas' (elements). According to the Yogaacaara 瑜伽行派 sect all elements are divided into the five ranks 五位 of: mind (心), elements possessed by the mind (心所), form (色), not corresponding with action (不相應行), not conditioned (無爲). Each of these five are subdivided. In mind (心) there are eight; in 'elements possessed by the mind (心所) there are fifty-one, among which are the five 'pervasive functions' (五遍行), the five 'special environments' (五別境), the eleven 'good elements' (十一善), the six 'primary defilements' (根本煩惱), the twenty 'secondary defilements' (二十隨煩惱) and the four indeterminate elements (四不定). In the group of form (色) there are ten, in the group that do not correspond with action, there are twenty-four, in the unconditioned, there are six. This altogether totals one hundred. The explanation of these elements appears in the Ta-sheng pai-fa ming-men lun 大乘百法明門論. 1 fasc., by Vasubandhu; trans., Xuangzang. T 1614.31.855b-c [Credit] acm(entry) cw(py)

百論 [py] Bai3 lun4 [wg] Pai (Po) lun [ko] Paengnon [ja] ヒャクロン Hyakuron ||| Bai lun; the `Sata-`saastra. 2 fasc., T 1569.30.168-182a. One of the three main treatises 三論 of the Maadhyamika school. It is attributed to Deva 提婆 and is thought to have been written down by Vasubandhu 世親. Originally translated by Kumaarajiiva 鳩摩羅什, there are various versions of the text, such as the Guangbai lun 廣百論. Its discussion centers on the refutation of the aatman 我 concept and other non-Buddhist ideas from the perspective of emptiness 空. [Credit] acm(entry) cw(py)
