習氣 [py] xi2qi4 [wg] hsi-ch'i [ko] sûpki [ja] ジッケ jikke ||| 'habit energies.' Latent karmic impressions created from all of the words, thoughts, actions and external influence experienced by sentient beings, closely related to the concepts of "seeds" --i.e. karmic impressions which help to comprise the total character of the individual entity. When it is discussed in distinction to seeds, it is understood to be the energy that exists even after destroying the seeds existent in the aalaya-vij~naana. According to the Yogaacaara school, the seeds and the habituated energies are removed during the period from the first bhuumi until the tenth. This energy is completely cut off at the time of attaining Buddhahood. (Skt. vaasanaa; Tib. bag chags) 〔唯識三十頌、 T 1586.31.61a; 成唯識論、 T 1585.31.43a; 瑜伽論T 1579.30.305b5〕(2) In his Ijangûi, Wônhyo notes two kinds of habit energies: distinctive 別習氣 and shared 反通習氣. 〔二障義HPC 1.793c〕 [Dictionary References] naka596b [Credit] acm(entry) cw(py)
