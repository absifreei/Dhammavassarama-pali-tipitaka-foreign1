體 [py] ti3 [wg] t'i [ko] ch'e [ja] タイ tai ||| (1) The body (gaatra). (2) The substance, or essence of something, The thing in itself. The thing in itself, distinct from its function (aatman). (3) Essence, substance, original nature (eka-artha). (4) Among the Three Greatnesses 三大, the Greatness of Essence. (5) Basic quality. (6) In East Asian Mahaayaana texts, usually refers to the Buddha-nature--to the original enlightened mind of sentient beings which may or may not be fully actualized in its function. [Credit] acm(entry) cw(py)

A grand-student of Toûi 道義, he helped to establish the Kaji-san school 迦智山 at Porim sa 寶林寺. [Dictionary References] ZGD807c [Credit] acm(entry)

體空 [py] ti3kong1 [wg] t'i-k'ung [ko] ch'egong [ja] タイ taikuu ||| 'essential emptiness.' The complement of 'analytical emptiness' Emptiness understood through the immediate apprehension of all existence being originally devoid of self-nature. [Credit] acm(entry) cw(py)

The realization that the original essence of all things produced by causes is empty, just as it is. The complement of 'analytical emptiness.' Considered to be a 'Mahaayaana view.'
